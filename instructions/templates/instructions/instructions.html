Instructions

What you will need:

  • [CORE] Minimum 1kg cut of meat
    • Topside cut of beef is preferred (extra fat for flavour), however, Silverside is just as suitable.
  • [CORE] Very sharp and durable, high-quality chef's knife
    • The better quality the blade, the cleaner and easier it is to cut the meat into strips.
  • [CORE] Vinegar
    • Malt vinegar. Balsamic and other vinegars can be used based on preference.
  • [CORE] Spices
    • Salt, pepper, and coriander.
  • [OPTIONAL] Spices
    • Bicarbonate soda to help soften the meat.white or brown sugar, dried garlic, paprika and chili flakes.
    • Bicarbonate soda to help soften the meat. flakes.
    • White or brown sugar.
    • Dried garlic.
    • Paprika.
    • Chilli flakes.
  • [CORE] Meat drying equipment
    • [MODERN] Electric food dehydrator. Very efficient, gives great results.
    • [TRADITIONAL] Wooden box with air holes, light-bulb (heat source), and small fan (air movement).

Steps:

  • Cut the meat into strips parallel to the long side of the meat.
    • Finger-width of your index finger.
  • Lay the meat in a deep dish or large bowl and liberally pour the vinegar over the meat.
    • Using clean hands, massage the meat with vinegar and ensure the vinegar covers the entire surface area of all the strips.
    • Leave some residual vinegar in the bowl for the meat to soak in.
  • Using a spoon sprinkle your spice mix over the meat.
    • Using clean hands, massage the meat with the spices and ensure the spice covers the entire surface area of all the strips.
  • Cover the dish or large bowl with cling-wrap and leave in cold storage for 24 hours.
    • Using clean hands, rotate and massage the meat every few hours.
  • Pull the strips of meat out the bowl and carefully position them in the dehydrator or drying box.
    • Be sure to leave space enough for air to pass easily over the meat.
  • Wait... patiently... for about 4 to 6 days.
    • You will notice the outer sides of the meat will go dark black - this is normal.
    • The fat will turn a deep brown-yellow - if good meat was used, it will be a rich colour and smell AMAZING.
    • During waking hours, every 3-4 hours, rotate the meat around to ensure uniform drying.
    • Feel the dry meat cuts after about 3 days, and every day after. They should start to become firm.
    • Based on preference, leave to dry hard (similar to well-done) or leave some softness for wetter biltong (similar to medium rare).
  • Slice the biltong. ENJOY!
